About Ayana

Ayana Renewable Power, established in 2017, is a leading renewable energy company in Bengaluru, India, owned by NIIF, BII, and GGEF. With 3.9 GWac capacity and a vision of 10 GWac by 2025, Ayana prioritizes sustainability and operational excellence.

Ayana Renewable Power has achieved a significant milestone by developing and managing over ~5GWac of solar, wind, and hybrid power projects in several Indian states.

Ayana's sustainability strategy aligns with its mission and vision, focusing on environmental impact, societal well-being, and governance. Rooted in UN SDGs, it aims to optimize energy usage, foster inclusivity, and drive ethical decision-making.

Contributing to UNSDGs

Ayana's sustainability strategy aligns with UN SDGs, focusing on SDG 7 for clean energy, SDG 8 for decent work, and SDG 13 for climate action. Through renewable energy generation, economic growth, and climate initiatives, Ayana makes impactful contributions.

Our Approach to
Stakeholder Engagement and Materiality Assessment

Ayana prioritizes stakeholder engagement, focusing on employees, investors, suppliers, community, regulators, media, and industry associations. A comprehensive materiality assessment identifies key issues like Occupational Health and Safety, ESG Governance, and Legal Compliance.

Environmental Stewardship
at Ayana

Ayana prioritizes environmental stewardship, with a 5-year track record, it focuses on achieving decarbonization, circular economy integration, and SDG alignment. Key achievements include significant solar energy production, waste recycling, and water conservation. The company implements an Environmental and Social Management System, adheres to international standards, and engages in biodiversity preservation.

Social System

Ayana prioritizes employees' well-being, ensuring fair compensation, and promoting a high-performance culture. The company invests in learning and development, maintains a diverse workforce, and upholds human rights, occupational health and safety, and responsible supply chain practices.

Governance

Ayana prioritizes robust governance, ethics, and policies, overseen by a diverse board. Its risk management relies on ERM, technology integration, and cybersecurity measures. Sustainability is integral, supported by a strong ESG framework.
